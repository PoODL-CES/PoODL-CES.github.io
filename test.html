In the Population and Organism Diversity Lab at the Centre for Ecological Sciences (CES), Indian Institute of Science, we study the diversity of populations of the species around us. We are especially interested in understanding how small populations survive or go extinct.

We conduct simulations and empirical studies on populations of wild animals to try and understand.

  • How do small isolated populations survive?
  • How do we design survival strategies?
  • Does genomic variation translate to phenotypic variation?

We also develop resources, tools, and techniques for genetic investigations of rare elusive species. We optimize methods for sequencing genomes from non-invasive and environmental samples.

Additionally, we ensure that the advances in wildlife genomic research are getting translated into on-ground management by conducting population genomics workshops across the tropics. Find the next scheduled workshop here.
